The 3D Cursor is this little red and white circle that starts at the object origin:

![](Pasted%20image%2020260403125658.png)

We can move the cursor around by pressing `Shift-RMB`. It will stick to the surface of meshes you click on by default.

When adding new objects, they appear at the 3D cursor.

We can use the 3D cursor as a temporary reference point for transformations by setting the pivot point at the top of the 3D viewport:

![](Pasted%20image%2020260403125859.png)
_Rotating around the 3D cursor, not the object origin._

It's can also be used with [Grease Pencil](VRC%20Character%20Book/Reference%20Topics/Grease%20Pencil.md) to define where the drawing plane is.
